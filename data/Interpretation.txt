1. Stock vs Nifty50 Performance Comparison

    Total Return
    - Stock return > Nifty return → Strong Candidate
    - Stock return ≈ Nifty → borderline
    - Stock return < Nifty → weak justification

    CAGR (Annual Growth)
    - CAGR > Nifty CAGR → consistent growth
    - CAGR >> Nifty CAGR → leadership stock
    - CAGR high but volatile → risky inclusion

    Volatility (Risk)
    - Slightly higher than Nifty → acceptable
    - Much higher than Nifty → risky
    - Lower than Nifty → very attractive

    Max Drawdown (Worst Fall)
    - Drawdown < Nifty → resilient stock
    - Drawdown ≈ Nifty → normal
    - Drawdown >> Nifty → fragile


    Red flags to avoid:
        High CAGR + very high volatility
        High total return + deep max drawdown
        CAGR only slightly above Nifty + much higher volatility
        Strong recent returns but weak 5-year performance
        Low volatility + low return
        Returns driven by one short explosive period
        Max drawdown much worse than Nifty during crashes
        Volatility rising while returns stagnate
        Good CAGR but long time spent in drawdown
        High CAGR but low absolute (total) return
        Similar volatility to Nifty but worse returns
        Better returns than Nifty but far worse drawdowns
        Outperforms only in bull markets
        Volatility spikes without return acceleration
        Return profile depends on a single event or rally

2. Trend Structure Analysis

    It checks whether the stock was long term leader or just a lucky performer

    Time above 200 day moving average
    - The percentage of days the stock price stayed above its 200-day moving average over 5 years.
    - Shows long term dominance
    - > 65–70% → strong index-quality trend
    - < 50% → inconsistent, not leadership material

    Longest Uptrend (Days)
    - The longest continuous stretch where price stayed above 200 EMA without breaking.
    - Represnts persitance, stability, confidence, liquidity of the stock
    - > 400–500 days → mature leadership 
    - Short streaks → late or fragile growth

    Average Correction (Average Drawdown)
    - Average Drawdown during the trend
    - How much it pulled back when it was uptrending
    - -10% to -25% → healthy growth corrections
    - <-40% → unstable, speculative behavior

    Trend Breaks
    - Number of times price went below 200 day EMA
    - Represents Trend Reliability
    - Lesser the Better
    - Frequent trend breaks = uncertainty
    - Low count (5–8) → stable long-term trend
    - High count (>12) → choppy, unreliable stock

    EMA 200 slope
    - Direction & steepness of the long-term trend.
    - Positive slope = trend is rising
    - Negative slope = long-term decline
    - Represnts Growth Maturity
    - Strong positive slope → growth phase
    - Flat slope → consolidation
    - Negative slope → declining relevance

3. Momentum and Strength Analysis

    Once the stock is structutally strong did it also show sustained momentum and strength which pushed it higher
    Road is solid but is the car accelerating on the Road
    Stock can be in uptrend but still move slowly or lose steam
    Nifty 50 include stocks which are not only stable but leader and can attract money
    Sustained bullish movements was observed or not
    Was the stock persistent and accelerating as time goes
    Persistence + Acceleration + Resilience 

    Strong stocks:
        Keep making progress
        Recover quickly after pullbacks
        Don’t stay weak for long

    Weak stocks:
        Stall
        Chop sideways
        Lose momentum after rallies

    Momentum Persistance (MACD)
    - Did the stock stay in bullish momentum regimes for long periods?
    - Was MACD mostly positive for years?
    - Positive MACD for long periods → leadership
    - Frequent flips → uncertainty
    - > 65%     ->  Sustained leadership
    - 50–65%    ->	Acceptable
    - < 50%     ->  Unreliable, late-stage

    Trend Strength (ADX)
    - Strength of the trend not the Direction
    - Strong trends attract ETFs, mutual funds, passive flows
    - > 25      ->	Strong trend
    - 20–25     ->	Emerging
    - < 20      ->	Sideways / weak
    - >50% of time if it was strong then its great

    Internal health of the trend (RSI)
    - During pullbacks, did strength reset quickly or break down?
    - How fast the stock recovers
    - Pull back -> Regain Strength -> Resume Trend ---> Healthy Stock
    - Pull back -> Stay Weak -> Loses Leadership ---> Unhealthy Stock
    - RSI > 40-50 ---> Strong Stock
    - RSI frequently dips into 30s ---> Weak Stock
    - > 55      -> Strong bias
    - 50–55     -> Acceptable
    - < 50      -> Weak

    RSI floor 10_pct : worst 10% of all time (5 years)
    - Higher values indicate controlled pullbacks 
    - If market s falling but still the stock is recovering pulling back how fast
    - > 40      ->	Healthy corrections
    - 35–40     ->	Acceptable
    - < 35      ->	Fragile / speculative
